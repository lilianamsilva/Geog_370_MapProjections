In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Description

I downloaded the Cultural Vector Layer off of Natural Earth. I then exported that layer into QGIS. I had already installed Indicatrix mapper as a pluggin into QGIS. This helped me in finding the cap and line segments for each projecttion. Next, I would click onto the project properties to see which projection I wanted the certain layer to be in. If I did not find the certain Authority ID right away, I would use the filter option. This would then limit my options. I would then click on my preffered/required projection and apply it to the map. This lead the layer to contort to the specified coordinates Last, I made it an image with the layer function and exported it as a png to my maps folder. I continued to do this for each projection.

WGS84 Projection

The WGS84 Projection has a linear scale.

Pros: Preserves the area, angles, and directions.

Cons: The area is distorted around the poles to try and keep the original length of each land.

Aitoff Projection

The Aitoff Projection is to take the shape of an ellipse.

Pros: The scale along the equator and meridian are correct.

Cons: Mostly everything is distorted as you move from the equator and meridian.

Psuedo Mercator

The Psuedo Mercator has a linear scale that is equal in all directions.

Pros: Preserves the angles and directions.

Cons: As we get further from the equator, the lands closer to the poles begin to distort causing unreliable areas.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant

The North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant is a projection from the North Pole.

Pros: All the angles between the lines are equal. The lines from the poles are true to real time.

Cons: The land distortion becomes greater as you grow farther from the poll.

Sphere Winkel I

The Sphere Winkel I projection is coordinates using the arithmetic mean

Pros: The curves and angles are equally spaced. The sizes and shape of the land areas are close to the sphere lands.

Cons: The sizes and lands are distorted as to try to keep the angles and distance.

World Cylindrical Equal Area

It is good for large-sclae mapping but not for small-scale. The areas surrounding the lines are closest to the same values.

Pros:It keeps the sizes equal of the areas.

Cons: It still distorts the land shapes and angles.

World Equidistant Conic

The World Equidistant Conic is good for regions that are in dimensions going from west-east.

Pros: The directions and distances are good towards the north pole.

Con: It does not show the whole "globe." This one gets cut off. There are extreme distortions to the hemispheres and areas.

Sphere Quartic Authalic

The Sphere Quartic Authalic is an equal-area map that is a distribution of spherical variables for the coordinates.

Pros: Equal area all through out the map and distances.

Cons: Distortion of lands.

Sphere Robinson

The Sphere Robinson is a very unique projection. It tries to use the least amount of distortion.

Pros: The distance latitude wise is close to the sphereical coordinates.

Cons: Distorts all areas: shape, area, distance, angles, and direction. The area distorition gorws with the latitude but does not change with the longitude.
